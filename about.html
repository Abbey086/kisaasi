School Philosophy

Kisaasi College School nurtures and cherishes the whole child. Emphasis is laid on excellent academic achievements as well as character building, participation in sports activities and developing leadership skills. Children should become responsible, self-driven and critical learners.

Academic Excellence
Strong teaching, revision and UNEB readiness.
Character & Morals
Integrity, respect, time management and discipline.
Leadership & Sports
Confidence, teamwork and student responsibility.

Vision

To train dependable and reliable citizens of the nation who can meet the challenges of today and tomorrow.

Mission

To provide all round education with diligence and accessibility to all.

Milestones & Growth

From a humble start with over 10 learners, Kisaasi College School has grown into a respected institution with over 900 learners and over 40 staff members, offering O and A Levels with recognizable performance.

Apply Now

  1. 1995 — Established

    The school began as a private mixed day and boarding institution.

  2. 2003 — Registered

    Registered with the Ministry of Education in Uganda.

  3. UNEB Center U1536

    Official UNEB center for both O and A Levels.

  4. Today — Continuous Improvement

    Upgrading professional practice, curricular resources, learning environment, and student outcomes— with students at the center stage.

Students Profile

There are over 900 students at Kisaasi College School. Learners come from across Uganda and beyond the borders. Admission is based on merit and qualifications from one grade level to the next.

New students are oriented through a well-planned programme to settle in, understand school culture, policies and guidelines. This sets a strong pace for learning. Our fees are middle range and affordable to most parents.

Orientation
School culture, policies & routines.
Merit-Based

Affordable

Teacher Profile

Teachers are role models and mentors for students. They guide learners through academic and co-curricular activities while laying the framework for independent work and leadership.

Teachers plan meticulously for all responsibilities in and out of class, aiming for standards aligned with the school mission. Professional development supports exposure to expected standards and practices. Staff are encouraged to be imaginative, creative and motivational.

Guidance and motivation.
Professional Growth
Continuous development.
